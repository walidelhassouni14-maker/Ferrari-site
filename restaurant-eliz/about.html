Our Beginnings

From Gaziantep to Berlin

Chef Mehmet Yılmaz grew up in Gaziantep, a city in southeastern Turkey renowned as the nation's culinary capital. From a young age, he watched his grandmother prepare slow-cooked stews, hand-roll börek, and craft baklava with the precision of an artist.

After training at the Culinary Institute of Istanbul and working in some of Turkey's finest restaurants, Mehmet moved to Berlin in 2010 with a dream: to bring the true taste of Anatolian cooking to Germany.

In 2012, Restaurant ELiZ opened its doors — named after his daughter Eliz, whose name means "unique" in Turkish. Over a decade later, it has become one of Berlin's most celebrated Turkish dining experiences.

Our Space

The Ambiance

Stepping into Restaurant ELiZ feels like entering a warm Turkish home. Hand-painted Iznik tile accents, lanterns suspended from a vaulted ceiling, and the gentle hum of Turkish folk music create an atmosphere of serene elegance.

We seat 80 guests across our main dining room, private booth area, and a heated terrace for seasonal alfresco dining. A private dining room for up to 20 is available for special occasions.

  • 80 seat main dining room
  • Private dining room (up to 20 guests)
  • Heated terrace, open April – October
  • Wheelchair accessible throughout
